A National Body (NB) typically refers to an organization, institution, or entity that operates at a national level. These bodies are established by the government or recognized by authorities to oversee, manage, regulate, or represent various aspects of national affairs. In this context the main task of a NB is to support the quality of mobility data and services by an assessment process.<br/><br/>In line with the legal framework of the ITS Directive 2010/40 and related Delegated Regulations a NB operates in the field of mobility. A NB is designated by a government and has to be impartial and independent from data providers and service providers. <br/>Two Delegated Regulations mention that a NB has to be appointed, namely regulation ‘Safety Related Information Services’ and ‘Truck Parking’.<br/>Suppliers of data and services shall/must submit a dataset, or a description of their data (metadata), to a National Access Point  including a ‘declaration of compliance’. This is called self-declaration.<br/>The NB will use these self-declarations as input in their assessment process to check if the quality of the dataset or service described is up to date and is compliant with the requirements of the Delegated Regulations. <br/>In some Member States a NB is authorised to impose a financial penalty to suppliers if quality is not up to date.<br/><br/>Abbreviations: <br/><br/>NB National Body <br/><br/>CA Competent Authority: An authority which is competent and authorized to fulfill this task aligned with  <br/><br/>the National Body <br/><br/>DR Delegated Regulation <br/><br/>FRAME the name commonly used for the European ITS Framework Architecture and is derived from the names            of the projects that since 2001 have developed its content and the methodology for its use. <br/>
